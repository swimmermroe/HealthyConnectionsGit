The Basics of Essential Oils

What are Essential Oils?

Essential Oils are the aromatic, volatile liquid that is within many shrubs, flowers, trees, roots, bushes, citrus rinds, and seeds. It’s the liquid that you see oozing from a plant if you cut or tear it. That plant liquid is responsible for transporting nutrients from the roots to the tips of the leaves and are rich in nutrients and compounds beneficial to health and vitality.

The chemistry of an essential oil is very complex and may consist of hundreds of different and unique chemical compounds.

The distillation process is what makes essential oils so concentrated.It takes, 5,000 lbs of rose petals to create 1 kilo of rose oil. Adding just one drop of peppermint oil to a glass of water has approximately the same concentration as 28 cups of peppermint tea.

Essential oils are different from vegetable oils that are greasy and sit on top of the skin. Vegetable oils also don’t have antibacterial properties and will oxidize and become rancid over time. In contrast, un-adulterated essential oils have naturally occurring chemical constituents that do not go rancid (unless altered by exposing them to heat for extended periods of time).

Essential oils come from a variety of plants.

5,000 lbs of rose petals go into 1kilogram of rose oil.

How are they made?

The method in which the oils are distilled may effect their chemistry and beneficial actions. Additionally, their chemistry will be different if they are from the second or third distillation of the same plant material.
